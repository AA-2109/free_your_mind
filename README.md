# How to Reclaim Your Brain

## *Free your mind and your ass will follow*

Modern apps, media platforms, and constant connectivity are designed to capture and hold your attention for as long as possible. Notifications, infinite scrolling, recommendation algorithms, and personalized feeds slowly train your brain to seek constant stimulation.

Over time, this can damage your ability to focus, reduce your attention span, increase anxiety, and make it harder to enjoy slower activities such as reading, thinking, or simply being bored.

This guide presents simple, practical steps to help you take back control of your attention. The goal is not to eliminate technology completely, but to **use it intentionally instead of letting it use you**.

Small changes - like removing apps, reducing notifications, and replacing doom-scrolling with reading or walking—can significantly improve your ability to concentrate, think clearly, and reclaim time in your daily life.

---

## Quick Rules

1. Remove all social media apps.
2. Stop checking the news every 5 minutes.
3. Avoid music streaming apps.
4. Avoid podcasts.
5. Disable YouTube history.
6. Use Pi-hole to block distracting sites.
7. Access distracting sites only on a desktop PC.
8. Start reading instead of doom-scrolling.
9. Use a minimalistic launcher on Android.
10. Disable unnecessary notifications.
11. Limit smartwatch notifications.
12. Get yourself an e-book reader.
13. Put your phone away while working.
14. Walk daily for at least 30 minutes.

---

## 1. Remove Social Media Apps

Social media apps are designed to keep you attached to your screen for as long as possible. Removing them from your phone can save you a surprising amount of time every day.

---

## 2. Stop Checking the News Constantly

Checking the news every few minutes is pointless. Important news will still be there later. If something truly significant happens, you’ll hear about it anyway. Checking once a day is usually enough.

---

## 3. Avoid Music Streaming Apps

Music streaming apps are slowly turning into social media platforms. Many are cluttered, buggy, and require a constant internet connection.

Your phone is more than capable of playing MP3 or lossless files locally. Alternatively, you can listen to FM radio. It’s free and can even help with loneliness.

---

## 4. Avoid Podcasts

Podcasts can easily become another passive time sink. Be mindful of how much time you spend on them.

---

## 5. Disable YouTube History

This may be one of the most powerful changes you can make.

The YouTube algorithm pushes you into an information bubble, feeding you the same content repeatedly. By disabling watch history, you force yourself to consciously decide what you want to watch and when.

No more clickbait.  
No more screaming thumbnails.

---

## 6. Use Pi-hole to Block Distracting Sites

Use Pi-hole or another network-level ad-blocking solution to block websites you can’t stop visiting on your own. Add them to a block list so they simply won’t load.

---

## 7. Use a Desktop PC for Distracting Sites

This is a simple but effective trick.

You can’t bring a desktop PC to the toilet or into a meeting. By accessing distracting websites only on your desktop, you break the habit of checking your phone every few minutes.

---

## 8. Start Reading Instead of Doom-Scrolling

Visit your local library and pick up a book. Start reading whenever you feel the urge to check your phone.

**Do not read on your phone.** Notifications will distract you and pull you back into old habits.

---

## 9. Use a Minimalistic Android Launcher

Install a minimal launcher to reduce visual clutter and temptation.

You can find many of them on **F-Droid**.

Examples include:

- Olauncher  
- KISS Launcher  
- Slim Launcher  
- mLauncher  

---

## 10. Disable Unnecessary Notifications

Spend 30 minutes disabling notifications you don’t actually need.

Keep only essential alerts, such as:

- Phone calls  
- SMS  
- Messaging apps for close family  
- Work email  

Disable everything else.

---

## 11. Limit Smartwatch Notifications

Smartwatches (like the Apple Watch) can become extremely distracting if every notification appears on your wrist.

Disable most notifications, or consider switching to a simple mechanical watch.

Unless you have a medical condition that requires monitoring, you probably don’t need all that data.

---

## 12. Get an E-Book Reader

If you live somewhere without easy access to libraries, consider buying an e-book reader.

Carry it with you and use it whenever you feel the urge to scroll on your phone.

---

## 13. Keep Your Phone in Another Room

When you are working or studying, keep your phone in a separate room. Removing it from reach dramatically reduces the temptation to check it.

---

## 14. Walk Every Day

Walk for at least **30 minutes every day**.

If possible, don’t bring your phone. If you want something to listen to, bring a small MP3 player or a portable FM radio instead.
